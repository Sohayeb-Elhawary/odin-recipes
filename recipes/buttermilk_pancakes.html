Buttermilk Pancakes

Today's gonna be a party, because today's another recipe for pancakes:

Credit: Dotdash Meredith Food Studios

Description

U have buttermilk in the fridge and don't know what to make of it? If yes so this pancake recipe is your savior.

Whether it's breakfast or a pre-workout, pancakes gonna be a great choice.

Buttermilk makes the pancakes fluffier than the typical ones.

It has good amounts of carbs, making it perfect in a pre-workout before training or gym.

NOTE! :

It contains rationally alot of carbs, be wary of the amounts eaten if you'll follow a cut diet.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 tablespoons white sugar
  • 3 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking soda
  • ¾ teaspoon salt
  • ¾ cup blue cheese, crumbled
  • 3 cups buttermilk
  • ½ cup milk
  • 3 large eggs
  • ⅓ cup butter, melted

Steps

  1. Combine flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt in a large bowl.

    Beat buttermilk, milk, eggs, and melted butter together in a separate bowl.

    Keep the two mixtures separate until you are ready to cook.

    Credit: Dotash Meredith Food Studios

  2. Heat a lightly oiled griddle or frying pan over medium-high heat. You can flick water across the surface and if it beads up and sizzles, it's ready.

  3. Pour the wet mixture into the dry mixture; use a wooden spoon or fork to mix until it's just blended together.

    The batter will be a little lumpy which is what you want.

    Credit: Dotdash Meredith Food Studios

  4. Pour or scoop batter onto the preheated griddle, using approximately 1/2 cup for each pancake. Cook until bubbles appear on the surface, 1 to 2 minutes; flip with a spatula and cook until browned on the other side.

    Repeat with remaining batter.

    Credit: Dotdash Meredith Food Studios

  5. Nice and Yummy love you fluffy!
